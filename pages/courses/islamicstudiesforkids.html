Islamic Studies for Kids Course

The early years of a child’s life are crucial for building strong values, identity, and faith. Our Islamic Studies for Kids Course is thoughtfully designed to introduce young learners to the core teachings of Islam in a fun, engaging, and age-appropriate way. Through interactive lessons, stories of the Prophets, daily duas, and essential Islamic manners, children will develop a love for their faith while building a solid foundation in Islamic knowledge.

Why Islamic Studies for Kids Course?

Build a Strong Islamic Identity: Children gain a positive and confident sense of being Muslim.
Develop Love for Islam: Through engaging stories, activities, and lessons, kids connect emotionally and spiritually to Islamic teachings.
Teach Core Values Early: Kindness, honesty, respect, and gratitude—Islamic studies nurture beautiful character from a young age.
Support at Home & School: This course complements both Islamic home education and full-time school learning.
Interactive Learning: Children enjoy visuals, quizzes, songs, and hands-on activities that reinforce their understanding.

What you will learn in this Course?

  • The Five Pillars of Islam and Six Articles of Faith
  • Stories of the Prophets (with lessons and morals)
  • Daily Islamic manners and etiquette (Adab)
  • Duas and short Surahs for everyday life
  • Basic Fiqh: Wudu, prayer, cleanliness, and good behavior
  • Importance of the Quran and Hadith
  • Understanding of Ramadan, Hajj, and other important occasions
  • Love and respect for Allah, the Prophet ﷺ, parents, and creation

Islamic Studies for Kids Course Outline

  • Module 1: Beliefs and Pillars
    • The Five Pillars of Islam
    • The Six Articles of Faith
    • Introduction to Tawheed (Oneness of Allah)
  • Module 2: Stories of the Prophets
    • Prophet Adam (AS)
    • Prophet Nuh (AS)
    • Prophet Ibrahim (AS)
    • Prophet Musa (AS)
    • Prophet Muhammad ﷺ (in child-friendly detail)
  • Module 3: Islamic Manners & Character
    • Respecting parents and elders
    • Speaking kindly and truthfully
    • Sharing, gratitude, and cleanliness
    • Kindness to animals and environment
  • Module 4: Daily Islamic Practices
    • Basic Fiqh: Wudu and Salah introduction
    • Learning daily Duas (waking up, eating, sleeping, etc.)
    • Simple Surahs with meaning
    • Importance of honesty and prayer
  • Module 5: Special Months and Events
    • Ramadan and fasting basics
    • Hajj and the story behind it
    • Eid celebrations and their meaning
    • Islamic calendar overview

Requirements

Suitable for children aged 5–12 (grouped by age level). No prior knowledge needed. Access to basic learning materials (Quran, notebook, pencils). A supportive learning environment (at home or in class). For online learners: a device with internet access and audio/video capability. Parental support and encouragement during learning
